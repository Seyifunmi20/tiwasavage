In the 2011 July issue of Exquisite Magazine, Tiwa Savage graces the cover and talks about sundry issues with the bold tag that a crush got him to pick music as a course in the Berklee College of Music. She told Exquisite that: "I decided to go into music because I had a crush on a guy in secondary school. I played [an instrument] in my hand at that time then but I realized that the singers got all the attention from the guys, so one day I told my instructor that I didn't want to play the trombone anymore, that I wanted to sing instead. He auditioned me and realized I was actually good so he put me in front with the other singers." After she dropped the instrument for singing, she informed that "Thing is, I didn't even manage to get the guys attention". Eya.. Pele!

"I learnt the power of music, it can make you happy just as it can move you to tears."
"It's important to me, as an artist, to use my gift to reflect the times we live in." "For me, my music is a journey without a destination, for myself and my fans. Where i am is where i'm suppose to be, and i believe every music will live on even after i'm gone

"A lot of people in Africa still have the ideal that a woman has to be submissive, stay at home and end be the wife and mother, Don't get e wrong, Those are great morals to keep, But i think the modern African woman, the modern black woman is being limitited, We can do both"
